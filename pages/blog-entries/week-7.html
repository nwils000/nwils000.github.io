Tired of Vanilla!

Javascript

This week with JavaScript has been a rollercoaster! I'm starting to feel the burnout from using plain vanilla JavaScript, but there's light at the end of the tunnel because we're jumping into React next week, which I absolutely love! The biggest takeaway for me this week is that almost everything in JavaScript is an object... functions, arrays, etc. This realization has shifted how I understand and follow JavaScript code, making unfamiliar features more approachable. I love JavaScript, but manual DOM manipulation isn't my favorite, which is why I'm so excited to start working with React!

The Job Search

Everyone keeps telling me that finding a job won't be a problem, but I can't help feeling skeptical. Is it just my insecurities? Despite these doubts, I'm pushing myself to network more actively, especially on LinkedIn. Just last week, I had lunch with the CEO of a local tech company. They aren't hiring junior developers right now, but things might change by the time I finish my bootcamp. We had a conversation, and he wanted to keep in touch. No promises, but it's given me a huge boost and the motivation to give my all to this bootcamp and my job search. Even though I'm naturally pessimistic, the experiences I'm gaining are slowly but surely helping me shed that negativity.
